phx.SphericalJoint

Spherical joint

Class  phx.SphericalJoint  |  Superclass: phx.base.Joint

Description

phx.SphericalJoint realizes a kinematic constraint with three degrees of freedom: free rotation about the X, Y and Z axes (a ball joint). The two bodies are pinned together at the given connection points but may rotate freely relative to one another.

Creation

Syntax

joint = phx.SphericalJoint(bodyA,bodyB) joint = phx.SphericalJoint(___,Name,Value)

Description

joint = phx.SphericalJoint(bodyA,bodyB) creates a joint between two bodies attached at their origins. Use PointA and PointB to set custom connection points.

Input Arguments

ArgumentTypeDescription
bodyA, bodyBphx.Body (scalar)The two bodies connected by the joint.

Properties

PropertyType / DefaultDescription
PointA[0 0 0]Connection point in the local space of body A.
PointB[0 0 0]Connection point in the local space of body B.
OverlayfalseDraw the joint as an overlay (always on top).

Reaction feedback (ForceA, TorqueA, ForceB, TorqueB) and the MutualCollisions switch (collisions between the connected bodies, default false) are inherited from phx.base.Joint.

Examples

Chain two arms with a ball joint

clf; view(3); axis equal; grid on; camlight headlight;

B = phx.Body("Position", [4 1 0]);
C = phx.Body("Position", [4 -2 0]);

phx.SphericalJoint(B, C, "PointA", [0 -1.5 0], "PointB", [0 1.5 0], "Color", 0.5);

sim = phx.Simulation;
sim.step(6, 600, 1);
delete(sim);
