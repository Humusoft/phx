PHX Toolbox

Object-oriented rigid-body physics for MATLAB®

PHX (the phx package) is a thin object-oriented layer over a rigid-body physics engine. A scene is built from phx.* handle objects that draw into MATLAB axes, are advanced by a phx.Simulation, and can optionally be driven from Simulink®.

A typical scene is only a few lines long: create one or more phx.Body objects, optionally connect them with joints, springs and force elements, wrap them in a phx.Simulation and call step.

% Minimal scene: a falling body and a static ground
clf; view(3); axis equal; grid on; camlight headlight;

phx.Body("Position", [0.6 -0.5 2]);   % dynamic box (default type and shape of a body)
phx.Body("Type", "static");           % ground

sim = phx.Simulation;                  % collect all bodies in the axes
sim.step(1, 100, 1);                   % 1 s, 100 substeps, redraw every step

Conventions. Constructors accept any public property as a Name, Value pair. Lengths are in metres, mass in kilograms, forces in newtons, torques in newton-metres and angles in radians unless stated otherwise. Positions, orientations and velocities are expressed in the world coordinate system; connection points and axes are given in each body's local coordinate system.
