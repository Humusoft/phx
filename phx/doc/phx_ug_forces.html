Forces, Actuators and Fields

Ready-made force elements beyond gravity and contacts

Beyond gravity and contacts, PHX offers ready-made elements for common types of interaction.

ElementWhat it doesKey options
Thruster An actuator rigidly attached to a body (rocket, propeller). Direction, MaxThrust, Throttle (−1…1)
Resistance Velocity-dependent environmental drag (air, water). VelocityFactors
Buoyancy Floating in a liquid — buoyancy and hydrodynamic damping. Density, Level, LinearDamping
Monopole Pairwise interaction of charges (gravity, electrostatics). Charge, Attractivity
Dipole Pairwise dipole interaction (magnets). Charge, Axis, Attractivity

Actuator — Thruster

clf; view(3); axis equal; grid on; camlight headlight;

body = phx.Body("Position", [0 0 1], "Shape", {"Capsule", "Axis", "z", "Density", 80});
phx.Thruster(body, "Point", [0 0 -0.9], "Direction", [0 0 1], ...
             "MaxThrust", 900, "Throttle", 1);
phx.Trace(body, "TracePoints", 240);   % motion trail

Gravitational/electric field — Monopole

% mutual gravity of a group of bodies, the built-in gravity is turned off
phx.Monopole(planets, "Attractivity", 6.6743e-11, "Charge", [planets.Mass]);
sim = phx.Simulation(planets, "Gravity", 0);
Tip. For floating bodies see phxex_buoyancy and phxex_capsize; for magnets phxex_magnets and phxex_maglev.
See also phx.Thruster, phx.Resistance, phx.Buoyancy, phx.Monopole, phx.Dipole
