phx.Buoyancy

Buoyancy and hydrodynamic damping

Class  phx.Buoyancy  |  Superclass: phx.base.Object

Description

phx.Buoyancy lets one or more bodies float in a liquid. The interior of each assigned body is sampled once by a deterministic regular grid of volume points. At every simulation step the points below the liquid level produce a buoyant force (Archimedes' principle) applied at the center of buoyancy — the centroid of the submerged sample points — so partially submerged bodies also receive the correct righting moment: a heeled hull rights itself, and bodies float at the draft given by their density ratio.

The liquid level is a horizontal plane at height Level, or an arbitrary time-varying surface defined by LevelFunction (e.g. waves). Optional velocity-proportional damping, scaled by the submerged volume fraction, approximates the hydrodynamic resistance of the liquid. A translucent, animated liquid surface can be drawn automatically.

Creation

Syntax

buo = phx.Buoyancy(bodies) buo = phx.Buoyancy(___,Name,Value)

Description

buo = phx.Buoyancy(bodies) creates a buoyancy element and assigns it to all given bodies. All assigned bodies float in the same liquid.

Input Arguments

ArgumentTypeDescription
bodiesphx.Body (1×n)Bodies the buoyancy acts on.

Properties

PropertyType / DefaultDescription
Density1000Density of the liquid, kg/m³ (default is water).
Level0Height of the liquid level, m.
LevelFunction[]Function handle @(x, y, t) returning the level height; overrides Level when set. Must accept arrays elementwise (use it e.g. for waves).
LinearDamping0Linear damping factor, N·s/m, scaled by the submerged volume fraction.
AngularDamping0Angular damping factor, N·m·s/rad, scaled by the submerged volume fraction.
Resolution8Number of sampling grid cells per axis of each body. Takes effect when the simulation pipelines are (re)built.
SurfaceCenter[0 0]Center of the drawn liquid surface, [x y].
SurfaceSize[10 10]Size of the drawn liquid surface, [x y]; set [0 0] to hide it.
SurfaceStep0.5Grid step of the drawn liquid surface.
SurfaceAlpha0.35Transparency of the drawn liquid surface (0 = invisible, 1 = opaque).

Limitations

The volume sampling sees only the outer visual mesh, so thin-walled or hollow shapes behave as if they were solid — model a boat hull as its solid (convex) shape. Open meshes (e.g. phx.shape.Terrain) are not supported. Bodies that are small compared to the sampling grid resolve the submerged volume in coarse steps and may bob between discrete equilibria; increase Resolution for them.

Examples

Floating and sinking bodies

% Half the water density -> floats half submerged; denser -> sinks
b1 = phx.Body("Position", [0 0 1], "Shape", {"Sphere", "Radius", 0.5, "Density", 500});
b2 = phx.Body("Position", [2 0 1], "Shape", {"Box", "Size", [0.5 0.5 0.5], "Density", 3000});

phx.Buoyancy([b1 b2], "Level", 0, "LinearDamping", 200, "AngularDamping", 30);

sim = phx.Simulation;
sim.step(5, 1000, 10);

Waves through a level function

% Travelling wave; the drawn surface animates along with it
phx.Buoyancy(b1, "LevelFunction", @(x, y, t) 0.15*sin(1.4*x + 1.9*t), ...
    "LinearDamping", 150, "SurfaceSize", [12 12], "SurfaceStep", 0.25);

See the phxex_buoyancy and phxex_capsize examples for a floating-bodies pool and a deck-cargo stability study.
