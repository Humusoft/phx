Measuring and Logging Data

Observing simulation results with Logger, Trace, Measure and Zone

Simulation results are observed with four helpers:

  • phx.Logger — records property values over time (position, velocity, force …).
  • phx.Trace — draws the trail of a chosen body point.
  • phx.Measure — measures the distance and relative velocity between two bodies.
  • phx.Zone — counts the bodies inside a box-shaped region and reports when they enter or leave it.
clf; view(3); axis equal; grid on; camlight headlight;

b = phx.Body("Position", [0 0 5], "Shape", {"Sphere"});
phx.Body("Type", "static", "Shape", {"Box", "Size", [6 6 0.3]});

% log the position at 100 Hz
log1 = phx.Logger(b, "Parameters", "Position", "Frequency", 100);

sim = phx.Simulation;
sim.step(3, 600, 1);
delete(sim);

% plot the recording
plot(log1.Time, log1.Data);
xlabel("time [s]"); ylabel("position [m]"); legend("x","y","z");

With multiple channels, individual signals are selected with log.getChannel(k).

A phx.Zone counts bodies inside a box-shaped region attached to an anchor body. It watches every moving body by default and can call back on entry and exit:

floor = phx.Body("Type", "static", "Shape", {"Box", "Size", [10 10 0.3]});
b = phx.Body("Position", [0 0 6], "Shape", {"Sphere"});

% a box region over the floor; report each body that enters
z = phx.Zone(floor, "Position", [0 0 2], "Size", [4 4 2], ...
    "EnteredFcn", @(zone, body) disp("inside: " + zone.Count));

sim = phx.Simulation;
sim.step(3, 600, 1);
disp(z.Count);         % bodies currently in the region
delete(sim);

For a pure end-of-run tally, make the zone passive with "SimulationOrder", "none" (it then costs nothing per step) and read it once after stepping with update(z).
