phx.assembly.chain

Chain of rigid links along a polyline

Function  phx.assembly.chain  |  Package: phx.assembly

Description

phx.assembly.chain stretches a link body between every two consecutive rows of an N×3 points matrix and connects the neighbouring links by joints at the interior points — a revolute joint around a given axis, or a spherical joint for a zero axis. With N points the chain has N−1 links and N−2 interior joints; pendulums, ropes with collisions and hanging chains are all special cases. Neighbouring links do not collide with each other (the connecting joint disables their mutual collisions), but the rest of the chain does collide with itself, so it can be piled up.

Creation

Syntax

parts = phx.assembly.chain(points) parts = phx.assembly.chain(points,Name,Value) parts = phx.assembly.chain(ax,___)

Description

parts = phx.assembly.chain(points) creates the chain in the current axes and returns a struct with the fields links (1×(N−1) phx.Body, the link body frame sits at the segment centre with its z axis along the segment), joints (cell row of the joints, interior joints first, then the anchor joints) and anchors (static mount bodies created by the Anchor option).

parts = phx.assembly.chain(ax,___) draws into the given axes instead of the current axes; an empty target ([]) creates the parts without graphics.

Options

OptionDefaultDescription
Shape"capsule"Link geometry: "capsule", "cylinder" or "box". A capsule is shortened by one diameter so that its rounded tips meet exactly at the joint points.
Diameter0.1Thickness of the links (the side length of a box link).
Axis[0 0 0]Rotation axis of the joints: a single 1×3 vector for all joints, or an N×3 matrix with one row per point (rows 1 and N are used only by the anchor joints). A non-zero row creates a phx.RevoluteJoint, a zero row a phx.SphericalJoint. Expressed in the same frame as the points.
Anchor"none"Pins an end of the chain to a static mount ball: "none", "start", "end" or "both".
Density1000Density of the link material (kg/m³); masses and inertias follow from the geometry.
Color[1 1 1]Common color of all parts.
Friction[0.5 0 0]Friction coefficients of all links.
Position[0 0 0]World position of the chain frame (in which the points and axes are expressed).
Orientationeye(3)World rotation of the chain as a 3×3 rotation matrix.
EulerAngles[0 0 0]World rotation of the chain as Euler angles (z→y→x order), an alternative to Orientation.

Examples

Double pendulum swinging in the x-z plane

clf; view(3); axis equal; grid on; camlight headlight;

parts = phx.assembly.chain([0 0 0; 0.4 0 0; 0.8 0 0], ...
    "Anchor", "start", "Axis", [0 1 0]);

sim = phx.Simulation;
sim.step(5, 2000, 5);
delete(sim);

Free chain of spherical joints dropped on the ground

clf; view(3); axis equal; grid on; camlight headlight;

pts = [zeros(10, 1), zeros(10, 1), (9:-1:0)'*0.2 + 0.5];
phx.assembly.chain(pts, "Diameter", 0.06);

phx.Body("Type", "static", "Position", [0 0 -0.05], ...
    "Shape", {"Box", "Size", [4 4 0.1]});

sim = phx.Simulation;
sim.step(3, 1200, 6);
delete(sim);
